FAQ

Straight answers about what Bearing sells, what it promises, and what your First Look result means.

Offers and pricing

What can I buy from Bearing?
Three things, and nothing else during the pilot: the free First Look, the First Bearing, and Continuity. If it isn't one of those, it isn't for sale.
What does the First Look cost?
Nothing. It's free — five questions and an instant directional read, with no advisor time and no obligation. Take it on the home page.
What does a First Bearing cost?
$1,500, one price. That covers the full assessment, advisor analysis, the 75-minute debrief, your One-Page Truth, and a 90-day Growth Map. There are no tiers and no add-ons.
What does Continuity cost?
$4,250, prepaid. It's a year of bearings: the First Bearing plus three lighter reviews through the year and an annual reassessment. Details on the Services page.
I already bought a First Bearing. Can I upgrade to Continuity?
Yes. Upgrade within 30 days of your First Bearing and the full $1,500 is credited toward Continuity — you pay the remaining $2,750. It's a package credit, not a stacked discount, so it doesn't combine with anything else.

Delivery, refund, and corrections

How fast do I get my results?
You receive your One-Page Truth within five business days of submitting your assessment, followed by the 75-minute debrief.
What if Bearing misses that window?
If Bearing misses the delivery window without an agreed reschedule, the First Bearing fee is refunded. That's the whole condition — no fine print.
What if something in my One-Page Truth is wrong?
Every First Bearing includes one free correction for factual errors reported within seven days of delivery. Tell us what's factually off and we fix it. The full terms are on the Services page.

Your First Look result

My First Look says “Adrift.” Is that bad?
No. Adrift is a directional state, not a score and not a shame label. It means the picture is blurry right now — the business hasn't been read clearly yet, which is common and fixable. It says nothing about how good the business is, only about how clearly it can currently see itself.
Is the First Look a grade on my business?
No. It reads direction — where the business stands on clarity, not how it ranks. There's no pass or fail, and no state disqualifies you from anything.

Fit

Who is a First Bearing for?
Owner-led businesses that want one honest page: three findings, one first move, and a measure to watch for 90 days. If you're willing to answer plainly and act on one thing, it fits.
Who is it not for?
Businesses looking for a long consulting engagement, a done-for-you implementation team, or a report that validates a decision already made. Bearing reads the business and hands you the first move; it doesn't run the business for you. If that's not what you want, the First Bearing isn't a fit — and we'll say so rather than sell it anyway.
Do you work with nonprofits?
Yes — nonprofits are a core focus. Bearing works on the operational side of finance: grant management (how you seek, report, and deploy funding), nonprofit financial systems (frameworks a board, an auditor, and a funder can all trust), and bank and lending relationships. It isn't tax prep or bookkeeping — it's the reading that shows where those systems are quietly costing you.

Privacy

Will my results be published?
Only with your separate written consent — never as a condition of the work, and never assumed from a signed engagement. No consent, no publication.
Who sees what I submit?
Your advisor, for the work itself. During the pilot the site also stays out of search engines, and we ask that you not submit sensitive client information through the contact form.

Booking

Is booking open?
Not yet. Booking goes live on the Services page the moment it opens — the button is there now, waiting. Until then, the free First Look is the way in.
