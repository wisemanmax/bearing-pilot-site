About

Bearing exists because most owners don't need more advice — they need to see their own business plainly. A bearing is not an opinion about where you should go. It's a reading of where you actually are, taken carefully enough to steer by.

The working idea

Put the whole business on one page the owner can read in a minute and act on for a quarter. Not a deck, not a retainer, not a framework with your logo on it — one page: three findings, one first move, one measure to watch. Everything Bearing sells is in service of that page being true, arriving on time, and being acted on.

Who runs it

Bearing is founder-led by Kameron Catron-Mitchell, MBA — an operations and business-execution leader with more than a decade in consumer finance: building and scaling operational functions at Sallie Mae and College Ave Student Loans, and now leading enterprise-wide execution of strategic initiatives as a Vice President at Wells Fargo. The through-line of that career is the same job Bearing does for clients — turning ambiguous goals into structured, repeatable operations: workflows, enterprise risk, regulatory compliance, and reporting that a board, an auditor, or a lender can trust.

That's also why the practice focuses where it does: financial systems and controls — the frameworks that make an organization legible; grant management and reporting — the same discipline of restricted money, clean books, and funder-ready reporting; and bank and lending relationships — the credit, covenant, and reporting expectations Kameron knows from inside the institutions themselves. Education: MBA, Business Analytics & Management Science, the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill; BS in Banking, Corporate Finance, and Securities Law, Delaware State University.

The pilots are new. The judgment behind the reading is not.

Principles the practice runs on

  • 01°A flashlight, not a lecture. The job is to light up what is already true in your business and hand you the evidence — not to perform expertise at you. If a finding can't be traced to something you said or a number you gave, it doesn't ship.
  • 02°Promise only what we control. Bearing promises a One-Page Truth within five business days and a 75-minute debrief, and refunds the fee if that window is missed without an agreed reschedule. It does not promise revenue, growth percentages, or outcomes that live in your hands, your market, or your luck.
  • 03°Plain language or nothing. If a finding needs jargon to sound important, it isn't a finding yet. You should never need a consultant to translate your own report.
  • 04°No manufactured dependency. The First Bearing is complete on its own. Continuity exists for owners who want their bearings kept current — it's a standing offer, never a pitch, and the report is built so you can act without us.
  • 05°Honesty about our own stage. The same clarity we sell, applied to ourselves — which is what the next section is.

Where the practice is right now

Bearing is new, and this page won't pretend otherwise. The practice is running its first five engagements in 2026 as the Founding Pilot Partner Program: the standard $1,500 First Bearing, granted to qualified organizations at no charge during validation, under the same delivery promise and correction terms published on the Services page. Every pilot is measured against fixed criteria — delivery time, whether the owner can restate their first move unaided, whether they act on it within 30 days — and the practice only scales if those numbers say it should. Client results will appear on the Success stories page only with each client's separate, explicit consent, after the pilot cycle completes. Until then, there is nothing borrowed, aggregated, or invented standing in for a track record.

Whether it's for you

Bearing works best for owner-led businesses and non-profits past the startup stage — real revenue or real funding, real obligations to owners, boards, funders, or lenders, and a nagging sense of being adrift: busy everywhere, sure of nothing. If that's familiar, the free First Look takes a couple of minutes and will tell you whether a full reading is worth your money. If it isn't, it will tell you that too.
