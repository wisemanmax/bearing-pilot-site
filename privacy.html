Privacy & data handling

This is a small pilot site, so this page can be short and exact. It describes what actually happens to information you give Bearing today — nothing more, nothing implied. If any line below stops being true, the line changes.

Internal honesty draft

This page must be reviewed by counsel before public launch

The site is unlaunched and marked noindex. This page is a plain-language account of our own data handling written for internal honesty, not a legal privacy policy and not legal advice. A lawyer must review and, where needed, rewrite it before Bearing goes public.

What is collected, and only when

Nothing is collected in the background. Information reaches Bearing only at the moment you choose to submit one of these things:

  • The contact form. Your name, email, and message — and only if you fill it in and send it. The form activates once the site's Supabase submission backend is configured; until then it stays disabled and nothing is sent.
  • The First Bearing assessment. A pilot ID (PILOT-01 through PILOT-05) and your answers about the business. There are no name or email fields on that form by design.
  • The First Look. An anonymized numeric score from 0 to 10 and its derived band — adrift, oriented, steady, or advancing. Nothing else: no answers, no name, no email, nothing that identifies you.
  • The Business Health Check. Anonymized 0–100 scores — one overall and one per dimension — when you score yourself. Your written answers never leave your browser; no name, no email, nothing that identifies you.
  • The booking request. Your name, email, timezone, preferred time windows, and optional business name and note — and only if you send the request form.
  • The client portal. For paid pilot clients only: logging in uses your pre-registered email (a one-time code, no password stored), and documents you upload go to a private folder that only you and Bearing can access. Nothing in the portal is readable by other clients or by the public site.

There is no analytics, no advertising, and no cookies. A few things are stored in your own browser's localStorage, and none of them leave your device except as described above:

  • Form drafts — the Health Check and the First Bearing assessment autosave what you type so a refresh doesn't lose your work; the draft clears once you submit.
  • Your portal sign-in — if you log into the client portal, your session (including the token that keeps you signed in and your email) is kept in localStorage until you sign out, so you don't have to re-enter a code on every visit.

All of it stays in your browser on your device; signing out of the portal or clearing your browser storage removes it.

Where it is stored, and who can read it

Submissions go to a database hosted on Supabase, in the United States. The public site holds only a write-only key: it can add a submission but cannot read anything back — not yours and not anyone else's. Row-level security permits insert and nothing more.

The only person who can read submissions is the founder, signed in to an authenticated dashboard. No one else has access, and the public key shipped to your browser cannot be used to retrieve stored data.

How long it is kept, and how to remove it

Submissions are kept for the duration of the pilot cycle so the founder can respond to and learn from them. If you want yours deleted, ask through the contact form and it will be removed.

What Bearing does not do

  • It does not sell or rent your information.
  • It does not run ads or advertising profiles.
  • It does not use tracking pixels or third-party analytics.
  • It does not share your information with third parties.
